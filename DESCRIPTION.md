# PERMANENT COLLECTION

An on-chain artwork that uses a speculative ERC-20 to build an immutable public collection of CryptoPunks via full-trait coverage. There is no deadline — the work completes only when all 111 CryptoPunks traits are represented by permanently vaulted Punks, or settles into an equilibrium where the remaining traits are held by owners who refuse the live bid.

**Artwork.** A single on-chain image: an 11×11 grid of all 111 CryptoPunks traits, rendered live from PunksData (the on-chain Punks pixel store at `punksdata.eth`). Each trait cell is in one of three states. **Permanent** traits render through the actual Punk that brought that trait into the vault. **In-return-auction** traits — currently in a 72-hour return auction — render with a distinct background, signalling that the outcome is undecided. **Uncollected** traits render as the isolated trait itself: just the hat, just the glasses, just the bald head — computed at read time by diffing the canonical Punk against a baseline. The image is recomputed from chain state on every read. No IPFS, no image server, no off-chain rendering. The artwork shows `FULL SET COMPLETE` only when every one of the 111 traits is permanent.

**The artcoin.** 111 is the artcoin for Permanent Collection: an ERC-20 launched through the artcoins protocol on Uniswap V4, native-ETH-paired. Trading the artcoin is the engine of the work — every trade pays a 6% baseline skim split inside the hook at swap-time across three destinations: **live bid 5%** of volume (funds Punk acquisition through Patron), **protocol 1.0%** (routed to PCController from block 1: 86.67% PC treasury / 13.33% LAYER burn), and an optional **referral ≤ 0.25%** pulled from the protocol slice when a swap carries valid attribution. With the separate 0.5% LP fee the all-in trading cost is 6.5%. No separate keeper, no per-swap callback contract — the hook routes the three legs directly. The MEV-window overage during the first ~30 minutes after launch (90% → 6%) routes 100% to the live-bid leg. A paced, permissionless V4 burn step periodically buys 111 and sends it to `0xdead` (fed by cleared-auction proceeds, not a trading-fee leg). The artcoin also carries a venue-scoped buy-side transfer tax (15% launch rate) that fires only when 111 leaves a *side* trading venue on a buy. The canonical pool is exempt, sells and wallet/lending/bridge/CEX transfers are never touched, and the tax accrues in the VaultBurnPool and is burned each time the protocol vaults a Punk. It blunts side-pool starvation by removing the incentive to route off-canonical, feeding the live bid indirectly through that routing shift. The artcoin does not redeem for vaulted Punks and does not control the vault.

**The live bid.** The protocol posts a single global ETH **live bid** for any eligible Punk. Any owner of a Punk carrying an uncollected trait can accept it: they list the Punk to the hub at a real price ≈ the current live bid (never 0), anyone then finalizes the acquisition, and the hub buys the listing through the market so the owner collects the listed price with `withdraw()`. Aligned listing contracts can also be allowlisted, so their public listings priced at or below the live bid can be accepted, with the caller earning a small finder fee. There is no atomic-acquire module and no comparable-listing rule — the live bid is the single acquisition path. `LiveBidAdapter.contribute(referrer, tag)` is the canonical on-chain destination for outside capital flows (NFT launchpads, wallet widgets, public-goods aggregators) that want to feed the live bid; the referrer receives a fixed slice, and the remainder buffers into the bid. `LiveBidAdapter` is the single inflow governor — every ETH source that funds the live bid enters the adapter and meters into the hub via its `sweep()` (the hub accepts ETH only from the adapter).

**Return auction.** Every acquired Punk enters a 72-hour **return auction** before custody is decided. The opening reserve is `paid × (101 + previousAttempts) / 100` — a 1% premium per prior attempt against that trait (first attempt = 1.01× the live bid paid), snapshotted at auction start. A bid in the last 15 minutes extends the deadline by an hour, uncapped: an actively-contested Punk stays in bidding indefinitely. If a bid above the reserve clears, the Punk goes to the high bidder via a `ReturnAuctionEscrow` provenance round-trip (so the canonical CryptoPunks market emits a real `PunkBought` event at the hammer price) and the proceeds split into fixed shares: 65% of the acquisition cost refills the live bid, 25% buys and burns 111, and 10% routes to a `VaultBurnPool`; the bid premium above cost flows to the same `VaultBurnPool` (less a fixed 5% slice to the high bidder's referrer when one is attributed), fueling future 111 burns on the next vault-path settle. Traits **do not** become permanent when an auction returns the Punk. If no bid clears, the Punk transfers to an immutable `PunkVault` — no withdrawal function, no admin override — and **only the recorded target trait** becomes permanent.

**Permanence.** "Permanent" means a Punk carrying that trait has entered the immutable vault. Acquisition alone is not permanence. Each cycle removes exactly one of two things from circulation: either 111 (return auction clears → ETH→111 burn) or a Punk (no bid → vaulted). The artwork is the sum of the Punks the market chose to retain in the vault — indefinitely, with no deadline.

**The Vault Title and the 111 Proofs.** `PunkVault` issues 112 named ERC721 objects, one for the work and one for each trait permanently brought into it. The **111 Proofs** (token ids 0..110, with token id matching the PunksData trait id directly — a Proof for trait 20 is token id 20) are commemorative artifacts minted at vault-settle to the seller-of-record whose Punk first brought each trait into the collection. The **Vault Title** (token id 111, just past the Proofs) is auctioned after the first 22 traits are collected, via `PunkVaultTitleAuction`; it names the steward of the work and grants no claim on the Punks, no withdrawal rights, no admin powers, no governance — purely a singular title record. Return auctions that return the Punk mint no Proof; redundant vaultings of already-permanent traits mint no Proof. The Title and the Proofs are both freely transferable. **Every vaulted Punk leaves a Proof.**

**Completion.** 111 traits is the entire CryptoPunks vocabulary across the four dimensions PunksData publishes: 5 normalized types, 11 head variants, 8 attribute counts, 87 accessories. The renderer flips to `FULL SET COMPLETE` only when every trait is permanent via vaulted Punks. There is no fixed endpoint — the work runs until the full set is complete, or until the remaining traits are held by owners who simply refuse the live bid.

**Stack.** Solidity 0.8.26, Uniswap V4 (artcoins V3 factory), a native-ETH-paired pool, a rewritten `ArtCoinsHookSkimFee` that splits the 6% baseline skim across three legs at swap-time (no keeper, no per-swap callback contract), a 72-hour return-auction module with provenance-round-trip clear-path settles, an immutable `PunkVault`, a paced permissionless V4 ETH→111 burn step, and a swappable on-chain renderer reading PunksData on every call. A 1-year auto-locking `ProtocolAdmin` gates a few bounded economic parameters and then freezes. A dormant composability surface (`PCSwapContext` + `PCNoReentry` decorations) preserves the option to bind synchronous third-party callbacks later — at launch, unbound; the flag is permanently false until a deliberate future binding. Frontend in Next.js (wagmi/viem); everything material lives on-chain.
